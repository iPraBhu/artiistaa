Bring the vibrant tradition of Madhubani art into your home with this exquisite painting created by master artist **Sita Devi** from the historic town of Madhubani in Bihar, India.

## The Artist's Story

**Sita Devi** is a third-generation Madhubani artist who learned this ancient art form from her grandmother. She has been painting for over 25 years and has won several state awards for preserving and promoting this traditional folk art. Each of her paintings is a meditation on the themes of nature, mythology, and feminine power.

## About This Painting

This piece depicts the traditional "Tree of Life" motif, surrounded by peacocks, fish, and geometric patterns that are characteristic of the Madhubani style. The painting represents abundance, fertility, and the interconnectedness of all living beings.

## Art Form Heritage

- **UNESCO Recognition**: Madhubani art was recognized by UNESCO in 1968
- **Ancient Origins**: Dating back to the Ramayana era (7th century BCE)
- **Women's Tradition**: Traditionally practiced by women during festivals and ceremonies
- **Natural Materials**: Uses natural dyes, pigments, and handmade brushes
- **Symbolic Meaning**: Every motif carries deep cultural and spiritual significance
