Discover the timeless elegance of Jaipur's famous blue pottery with this stunning decorative plate, handcrafted by **Mohanlal Prajapat**, a master potter whose family has been preserving this art form for seven generations.

## The Master Potter's Legacy

**Mohanlal Prajapat** learned this ancient craft from his grandfather in the narrow lanes of Jaipur's old city. His workshop, established in 1952, has trained over 100 artisans and is recognized by the Government of Rajasthan for preserving this traditional art form.

## About Blue Pottery

Jaipur Blue Pottery is unique because:

- **No Clay**: Made from quartz, glass, and fuller's earth
- **Persian Origins**: Brought to India by Mughal artisans
- **Cobalt Blue**: The signature blue comes from imported cobalt
- **High-Fire Technique**: Fired at temperatures over 1000°C
- **Lead-Free**: Completely safe for food use

## The Craft Heritage

- **16th Century**: Introduced during Akbar's reign
- **Royal Patronage**: Favored by Rajput maharajas
- **UNESCO Status**: Recognized traditional craft
- **Pink City Pride**: Symbol of Jaipur's artistic heritage
- **Women Artisans**: 60% of painters are women
