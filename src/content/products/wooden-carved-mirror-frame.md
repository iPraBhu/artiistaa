Transform your space with this magnificent handcarved mirror frame, a masterpiece created by **Rajesh Kumar**, a fourth-generation woodcarver from Saharanpur, Uttar Pradesh - the woodcarving capital of India.

## The Artisan's Legacy

Rajesh Kumar comes from a family of master woodcarvers who have been preserving this ancient craft for over 100 years. His great-grandfather was a court artisan for the Mughal nobility, and this rich heritage flows through every chisel stroke in this exquisite piece.

## About This Mirror Frame

This stunning mirror frame showcases the intricate "Jali" (lattice) work and floral motifs characteristic of Mughal-era design. The frame features:

- **Traditional Motifs**: Hand-carved lotus flowers, vines, and geometric patterns
- **Premium Wood**: Made from sustainably sourced sheesham (Indian rosewood)
- **Masterful Craftsmanship**: Over 40 hours of meticulous hand carving
- **Natural Finish**: Sealed with traditional wood polish for longevity

## Saharanpur Woodcraft Heritage

- **500+ Years**: Tradition dating back to the Mughal period
- **Royal Patronage**: Historical connection to Mughal courts
- **UNESCO Recognition**: Part of India's Intangible Cultural Heritage
- **Sustainable Practice**: Uses locally grown sheesham wood
- **Women's Participation**: Supporting 200+ women artisans in finishing work

## Care Instructions

- Dust regularly with a soft, dry cloth
- Avoid direct sunlight to prevent wood darkening
- Polish annually with natural wood oil
- Humidity should be kept between 40-60%

This purchase supports **Sunita Devi** and her team of 12 women who handle the intricate finishing work, including sanding, polishing, and quality inspection. These women earn 40% above minimum wage and receive health insurance for their families.
