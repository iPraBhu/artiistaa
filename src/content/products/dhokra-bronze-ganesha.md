## About Dhokra Art

Dhokra is one of the world's oldest metal casting arts:

- **Ancient Legacy**: 4000+ years old, dating to Harappan civilization
- **Tribal Wisdom**: Practiced by indigenous communities
- **Lost-wax Process**: Each piece is unique, mold destroyed after casting
- **Sacred Tradition**: Originally for temple rituals and deity worship
- **Zero Waste**: Completely eco-friendly process

## Traditional Dhokra Process

The 12-step ancient technique:
1. **Clay Core**: Made from riverbank clay and rice husk
2. **Wax Sheets**: Beeswax mixed with tree resin
3. **Intricate Detailing**: Hand-carved patterns and textures
4. **Clay Mold**: Multiple layers of special clay
5. **Bronze Melting**: In traditional furnaces at 1200°C
6. **Pouring**: Molten metal replaces wax
7. **Cooling**: Natural air-drying for 24 hours
8. **Breaking**: Mold destroyed to reveal unique piece
9. **Filing**: Hand-finishing with traditional tools
10. **Polishing**: Natural abrasives for smooth finish
11. **Patina**: Ancient oxidation techniques
12. **Final Blessing**: Consecrated by tribal priest
